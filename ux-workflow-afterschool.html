NarrowedProblem brief
Problem brief
Parents don't lack activity options — they lack a trustworthy way to know if an activity is actually good, because existing sources are either from strangers or from the business itself, never from parents like them.
Why this won: 5 of 8 interview quotes and the review-aggregator gap all converge on the same missing ingredient — verification from a peer, not a stranger or a seller. The structure/time-poor threads are real but smaller — parked, not dropped.
Carries into Synthesis: all 8 quotes, the market gap map, and the 5 Whys chain — ready for clustering.

NarrowedEpic & stories
Epic
Bring word-of-mouth trust to activity discovery
● As a parent researching activities, I want to see reviews only from parents whose kids actually attended, so I can trust the feedback is real.
● As a parent, I want to filter listings by "most reviewed by parents near me," so I find options my own community already trusts.
● As a reviewing parent, I want my review tied to verified attendance, so other parents know it's credible.
Success metric
% of early-access signups who go on to browse at least 3 verified listings — validates the trust signal before building the full platform.
Carries into Design: the scoped spec + the validation metric. Won't-items are parked in the backlog — revisit after the pilot signal.

Structural change
Verified parent reviews sit directly on the activity detail page — not in a separate reviews tab.
Why this won: it's the core trust signal, not a secondary feature — burying it defeats the entire HMW.

Raw dataWireframe iterations & usability tests
v1
Generic star rating only, buried in a "Reviews" tab. Finding: parents said "could be anyone" — no trust gained.
v2
Added "verified attendee" badge, still in a separate tab. Finding: better trust, but parents didn't scroll far enough to see it.
v3
Verified review count + top review shown directly on the main detail view. Finding: parents immediately said "that's from people who actually went — I believe it."
NarrowedValidated prototype
Validated prototype
v3 flow — verified reviews surfaced immediately on the activity detail page, no extra click.
Why this won: the only version where the trust signal was seen instantly — matching how word-of-mouth trust actually works: immediate, not researched.
Loop closes: this ships as an early-access landing page + limited neighborhood pilot, measured against the validation metric. Since no product existed before this, success means "enough signal to build the real thing" — not an improvement on an existing number.
