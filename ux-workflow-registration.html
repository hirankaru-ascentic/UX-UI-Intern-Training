FramingDefine the Problem Space
"We are solving why prospective learners abandon account creation before completing it — for users still deciding if the platform is worth their time, not yet committed learners."
NarrowedProblem brief
Problem brief
Users abandon signup before seeing course value — because the platform asks for personal details before showing anything worth committing to.
Why this won: 5 of 8 interview quotes, 3 of 4 competitor gaps, the single biggest funnel drop, and the top survey reason (61%) all converge on the same moment and cause. The other threads (trust/spam concern, form length, fit uncertainty) are real but smaller — they carry forward as minority themes, not dropped.
Carries into Synthesis: all 8 quotes, the competitor gap map, funnel data, and survey results — nothing discarded, everything ready for clustering.

NarrowedEpic & stories
Epic
Show proof of value before requiring signup
● As a prospective learner, I want to watch one free lesson without logging in, so I can judge if the course is worth my time.
● As a prospective learner, I want to see how many lessons are in the full course, so I understand the value of signing up.
● As a returning visitor, I want to be prompted to sign up only after finishing the preview, so I'm not interrupted mid-value.
Success metric
Registration completion moves from 28% toward 45% on the test cohort vs. control.
Carries into Design: the scoped MVP spec + the metric that will prove or kill it. Won't-items are parked in the backlog, not deleted — revisit after the test result.

NarrowedIA decision
Structural change
Preview lesson player is inserted between Course detail and Signup — not after it.
Why this won: matches the MVP scope's Must item exactly — signup only appears once the preview is watched, not before.

This exact flow and IA structure is what the wireframes below are built against — step 5 is the one moment the whole MVP scope exists to protect.
Raw dataWireframe iterations & usability tests
v1
Auto-play preview, no controls. Finding: users didn't realize they could skip or pause — felt trapped.
v2
Added play / pause / skip controls. Finding: better, but the signup prompt interrupted mid-lesson — felt like a bait.
v3
Signup prompt only after the lesson ends, "Continue to full course" CTA. Finding: users completed the preview and moved to signup naturally.
NarrowedValidated prototype
Validated prototype
v3 flow — preview with playback controls, signup CTA appears only after completion.
Why this won: the only version where users didn't feel interrupted or trapped, across every usability session.
Loop closes: this ships as a live A/B test against the original goal — 28% → 45% registration completion. If it moves the number, the assumption is proven. If not, the loop returns to Discover, not to a redesign of the same idea.
